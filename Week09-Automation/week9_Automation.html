Things to consider

What are your outputs? Do you want them to look similar?

Does your tooling enable you to share formatting between outputs?

Do you want to set it up semantically or by display?

Are there accessibility/usability considerations?

Middlesex Community College - Tools and Technologies for Tech Writers - Winter 2022

Semantic vs Display

Semantic - Create styles based on use.

You may have many styles that look the same, but if a style changes it might be easier to update.

For example: uicontrol, wintitle, userinput, keyboard

Display - Create styles based on formatting.

One style per display, but user's have to remember to apply the right ones.

For example: bold, monospace

Template maintenance

Even if you never make a template, you will use one.

  1. If someone tells you to update, update. Do not wait.

    Either you will miss something important, including legal updates, or you start building technical debt.

  2. If you discover old stuff, tidy as you go.

    Again, cleaning up technical debt is really, REALLY worth it.

Outdated templates can cause you lots of avoidable headaches.

Also helps out your descendents. When you pass on your project, they don't have to guess which of the 7 similar cross-reference formats to use.
