Basic Chocolate Truffle:

8 Oz. Dark sweet chocolate

1 cup heavy cream

Cut chocolate into small pieces. Place in top of double boiler together with the cream. Place hot (not boiling) water in the bottom of the double boiler and stir until melted and well combined. Remove from heat and cool to room temperature. Transfer to covered bowl and place a layer of plastic wrap directly onto the surface of the ganache and up the sides of the bowl to prevent water from condensing onto it. Refrigerate overnight to harden.

Chocolate Caramel Truffles

Makes about 30 truffles.

1 cup sugar

2/3 cup heavy cream

9 ounces fine-quality bittersweet chocolate (not unsweetened), chopped

1/4 teaspoon salt

1 teaspoon vanilla

Day 1:

In a dry heavy saucepan (about 3 1/2 quarts) cook sugar over moderate heat, stirring with a fork until melted, and swirl pan until sugar is a golden caramel.

Remove pan from heat and add cream carefully (mixture will bubble up). Return pan to heat and simmer, stirring, until caramel is dissolved.

Remove pan from heat and add chocolate, salt, and vanilla. Let mixture stand 5 minutes and stir until chocolate is melted. Transfer mixture to a bowl and cool, uncovered. Chill mixture, covered, 2 hours, or until firm.

Later on Day 1, or Day 2:

Scoop out truffle mixture with a spoon and form into 1-inch balls.

Dip, or roll truffles in 1/2 cup cocoa powder or coat with 1 cup pecans, pressing nuts slightly to adhere. (Alternatively, roll half of truffles in 1/4 cup cocoa powder and coat remaining truffles with 1/2 cup pecans.)

Chill truffles on a tray lined with wax paper until firm, about 1 hour. Truffles keep in an airtight container, chilled, 2 weeks.

Coconut Macadamia Truffles

A triple batch makes about 114 centers. This required less than an 8 oz. bag of coconut.

8 oz Lindt, Callebaut or Ghirardelli white chocolate (not morsels)

1 cup salted dry-roasted macadamia nuts (5 oz) – lightly toasted

1/4 cup heavy cream

2 tablespoons dark rum

1 1/2 cups finely shredded unsweetened desiccated coconut

Finely grind white chocolate in a food processor (or finely chop).

Pulse nuts in food processor until coarsely chopped.

Bring cream to a simmer in a double boiler.

Whisk in white chocolate until melted and ganache is smooth

Remove from heat and whisk in rum until well blended.

Stir in nuts.

Pour ganache into a plastic-wrap–lined 8-inch square baking pan (or use regular method, without plastic).

Cool to room temperature.

Chill, until firm, about 4 hours.

Invert ganache onto a work surface and remove plastic wrap. Cut ganache into 64 squares and roll each piece between your palms to form a ball. (or, use scooper to form centers).

When all balls are formed, roll in coconut to cover completely.

Dip

Top with more shredded coconut.

Alternative: Form centers without coconut, dip, and then roll in coconut.

Chocolate Truffles with Pecans and Dried Cranberries

Makes about 48.

Bon Appétit, December 2001

3/4 cup whipping cream

3 tablespoons unsalted butter

2 tablespoons Lyle's Golden Syrup, light corn syrup, or brown rice syrup

4 tablespoons frozen concentrated cranberry juice cocktail, thawed

16 ounces bittersweet (not unsweetened) or semisweet chocolate, chopped

1/2 cup dried cranberries

1/2 cup pecans, toasted, coarsely chopped

Stir cream, butter, syrup, and cranberry concentrate in heavy large saucepan over medium-high heat until mixture comes to boil. Remove from heat. Add chocolate and whisk until melted. Mix in cranberries and pecans. Pour truffle mixture into 11x7-inch glass dish. Chill until firm.

Scoop truffle mixture with melon baller or spoon and roll between palms to make round centers. Place on baking sheet. Return to refrigerator or freezer to chill before dipping.

White Chocolate Anise Truffles

12 oz Ghirardelli white chocolate, chopped coarsely

1/3 Cup whipping cream

2 Tbsp. Pernod

1/8 to 1/4 teaspoon anise oil

In a double boiler, melt white chocolate with whipping cream over low-medium heat, stirring constantly.

Remove top of double boiler with the melted chocolate from the heat.

Whisk in liquor until blended. Add anise oil a drop or two at a time, tasting to get the desired amount of anise flavor.

Pour into a pie plate or bowl and cool until room temperature.

Chill completely.

Shape into balls for dipping.

Whiskey Raisin Truffles

Makes about 4 dozen truffles

1/2 cup raisins, chopped

1/4 cup plus 2 Tablespoons whiskey

1 cup whipping cream

10 ounces semisweet chocolate, finely chopped

3 Tablespoons sweet butter, small pieces

Day 1:

Soak raisins in whiskey in small bowl overnight.

Day 2:

Heat cream in medium-sized saucepan to simmering. Remove from heat. Add the chocolate and the butter. Whisk until smooth. Cool to room temperature. Stir in raisins and whiskey. Refrigerate, covered, stirring occasionally, 3-4 hours or overnight.

Day 3:

Use melon baller to scrape hardened chocolate mixture from pan. Place on waxed-paper covered cookie sheet. Place full pan in freezer while completing this stage.

Keeping centers cold, quickly shape into 1-inch balls. Return balls to freezer and chill before dipping.

Dip

Cognac-Walnut Truffles

This makes about 100 pieces

Ganache:

24 oz. dark chocolate (Callebaut or Ghirardelli are best)

1 cup heavy cream (not whipping cream)

6 Tablespoons Cognac (I use Stock brand brandy)

200 Walnut Halves (I get them at Trader Joe's)

In double-boiler, melt chocolate together with cream, heating gently

and stirring until well blended, smooth and homogeneous. Cover bowl

with plastic wrap, making sure plastic touches surface of ganache all

across to seal out water and air. When ready, form ganache into

balls about the size of a marble or slightly larger, and sandwich each

ball between two walnut halves.

Chocolate-Hazelnut Truffles

Makes about 4 dozen truffles

1/2 cup hazelnuts

9 oz semisweet or dark sweet chocolate, broken into small pieces (NOT chocolate chips)

1 c heavy cream

4 tbsp unsalted butter

3 tbsp Frangelico liqueur

Day 1:

Heat oven to 350°F.

Toast hazelnuts in shallow baking pan 15 minutes, shaking pan occasionally. Transfer nuts to coarse sieve; rub around, using cloth to protect your hand, until skins have come off. Let cool completely.

When hazelnuts have cooled, select 48 nuts of uniform size; store in tightly covered container, in refrigerator, for later use as decoration. Chop remaining nuts coarsely (you should have about 1/4 c).

Place chocolate, cream, and butter in top of double boiler; heat over simmering water, stirring often with wooden spoon. When chocolate has melted, beat with spoon until smooth. Remove from heat; let cool to room temperature.

When ganache is cool, stir in Frangelico and chopped hazelnuts until well combined and transfer to medium bowl. Refrigerate, covered with foil, overnight or up to several days to allow ganache to ripen and harden.

Day 2:

Working with a small amount of the ganache at a time and keeping remainder refrigerated, form into balls about 1 inch in diameter, using about 1 tsp of ganache per ball; work with fingertips, not palms of hands, to keep ganache from softening too much. Place on baking sheet lined with waxed paper. When sheet is full, refrigerate until truffles are somewhat firm (about 1 hour), then cover loosely with waxed paper and refrigerate until quite firm and set (1-2 days).

Day 3 or 4:

Dip

Chocolate Espresso Kahlua Truffles

Adapted from a recipe in Gourmet, February 1996

Makes about 40 truffles.

12 ounces fine-quality semisweet chocolate, chopped

1/2 stick (1/4 cup) unsalted butter, cut into pieces

1/4 cup heavy cream

1 tablespoon instant espresso powder dissolved in 1 tablespoon boiling water

4 tablespoons Kahlua (original recipe calls for 2 tablespoons Sambuca or other anise liqueur)

[about 1 cup unsweetened cocoa powder for coating truffles]

In a double boiler or in a metal bowl set over a pan of barely simmering water melt chocolate and butter with cream and espresso, stirring until smooth. Remove top of double boiler or bowl from heat and stir in liqueur. Cool mixture and chill, covered, at least 3 hours, or until firm.

Form rounded teaspoons of mixture into balls [and roll balls in cocoa powder, coating them]. Truffles keep, layered between sheets of wax paper and chilled, in airtight containers 2 weeks.

(Note: Several reviewers at the Epicurious.com site said that this is a great base recipe for adding any kind of liqueur. It does seem to take a larger amount of liquor without yielding an overly soft ganache, perhaps because of the butter.)

Mexican Molé Truffles

Makes about 25 truffles

1/4 tsp. chipotle chile powder

1/2 tsp. cinnamon

1/2 tsp. coriander

1/2 tsp. allspice

1 pinch nutmeg

1 pinch cumin

Truffles Au Paprika (Deviled Chocolate Truffles)

(To get about 96 truffles, I multiplied this recipe times 5.)

5 ounces unsweetened chocolate (I used semi-sweet)

1 cup cream (heavy)

1 1/2 ounces prunes

1 ounce armagnac

1/2 tablespoon cocoa powder

1/2 tablespoon paprika, sweet (or mild)

Warm the Armagnac and soak the pitted and roughly chopped prunes in the brandy until they are soft.

Melt the chocolate.

Bring the cream just to a boil and take off heat and mix into the melted chocolate.

Allow to cool.

Finely chop the prunes and add the prunes and any unabsorbed brandy into the cooled chocolate mixture. (Next time I might use the cuisinart to chop prunes. I tried to chop fine with a knife, but they were still a bit chunkier than I wanted.)

Chill until firm but not hard.

Mix cocoa powder and paprika on flat plate. Roll the chocolate mixture into small balls and roll each ball in the cocoa/paprika mixture.

Store in a cool place.

NOTE: I did not roll the balls in paprika. I mixed paprika into the chocolate/cream mixture because we were going to dip the truffles. Maybe we should try some truffles just rolled in cocoa or other things next year.

NOTE 2: I was worried about this recipe that I pulled from the Internet. The proportions of the cream to chocolate did not seem to match other recipes. I did end up adding more chocolate to the mixture because I felt that it would not congeal properly. HOWEVER, next time, I think I might just try following the recipe exactly because I think the paprika truffle centers are a bit hard.

NOTE 3 (from Natalie, 2007): Following the recipe exactly yielded a ganache that never firmed up enough to roll, so I changed the proportions and added some butter:

Honey Spice Truffles

(Multiplied recipe times 5)

5 ounces semi-sweet chocolate, finely chopped

1/2 cup whipping cream

1/4 teaspoon vanilla or 1/4 vanilla bean (split and scraped) Note: I always use double strength vanilla.

1/2 cinnamon stick

1 whole clove

1 1/2 tablespoons honey

Pinch of salt

1/2 tablespoon unsalted butter, room temperature

8 ounces bittersweet chocolate

Place the semisweet chocolate in a medium-sized mixing bowl and set aside.

In a small saucepan, combine the cream, vanilla, cinnamon, clove, honey, and salt. Bring just to a boil over medium heat; remove from heat. Pour 1/2 the cream through a sieve over the chopped chocolate and let sit for a minute. After 1 minute, gently stir the cream and chocolate mixture with a spatula, starting in the center and slowly working toward the outside of the bowl (in a slow circular motion). Remove cinnamon stick and clove and discard. Add the remaining cream and stir as directed above, then stir in the butter. The molten chocolate, cream, and butter will blend slowly, then become smooth and glossy (this glossiness is a good sign that the suspension is stable). Cover the bowl with plastic wrap, pressing the film onto the surface of the chocolate to prevent a thin skin from forming. Refrigerate until firm, approximately 1 hour.

Remove the chilled ganache from the refrigerator. Scout out 1/2 ounce balls of the chocolate (about the size of a Bing cherry) and place them on a baking sheet covered with either parchment or wax paper; return them to the refrigerator.

Dip the truffles and then toss into the cocoa powder or decorate as desired.

2007 Tip from David R: mixing the cream and chocolate bits in a bowl set over

a pot of hot water to helped it melt.

Double-Decker Mint Patties

Mint enhanced white and dark chocolate are layered--then dipped in more

chocolate--in this after-dinner candy.

Makes about 16 (I multiply by 6 for trufflemania)

1/4 cup plus 2 tablespoons whipping cream

2 tablespoons (1/4 stick) unsalted butter

6 ounces semisweet chocolate, finely chopped

7 drops of peppermint oil (available at Michael's or a candy supply store)

6 ounces imported white chocolate, finely chopped

2 teaspoons sour cream

Line 9.5 x 5.5-inch loaf pan with foil. Bring 1/4 cup cream and butter to boil

in heavy small saucepan. Reduce heat to low. Add 6 ounces semisweet chocolate and stir until melted and smooth. Mix in 4 drops of peppermint oil. Pour into prepared pan. Freeze until firm.

Bring remaining 2 tablespoons cream to boil in heavy small saucepan. Reduce

heat to low. Add white cocolate and stir until melted and smooth. Remove from

heat and stir in sour cream and remaining 3 drops of peppermint oil. Cool to

lukewarm. Pour over dark chocolate layer. Smooth with back of spoon. Tap pan

on counter even. Freeze until firm, about 1 hour.

Line baking sheet with waxed paper. Lift chocolate from pan using foil sides

as aid. Cut candy into 1 1/4-ince rounds, using cookie cutter dipped into hot

water and wiped clean between cuts. Set rounds on prepared sheet. Freeze until

very firm, about 1 hour.

Dip and decorate.

Bon Appetit, December 1988

NOTES:

--These were introduced to me as squares and I've done them as squares.

--Because it is hard to find 6 loaf pans, I always use other containers, but

that means that they aren't completely uniform.

--These are hard to cut. If they are too cold, they crack or separate. If they

are too warm, they stick.

--Always warm up the chocolate layer just a bit before pouring on white

chocolate because otherwise they won't adhere properly.

--I like the dots we used as decoration this year. They were fun.

Pomegranate Truffles

8.5 oz. bittersweet chocolate (I used Ghirardelli 60% chips)

1/2 cup + 1 Tbsp. Pomegranate syrup

1 tsp. vanilla

Melt chocolate. Heat pom syrup to ~110°. Stir syrup and vanilla into melted chocolate and combine thoroughly. Ganache will smooth out with stirring. Let ganache firm up overnight in the refrigerator. Ganache will be sticky and may need to be partially frozen to fully shape.

Makes about 25 truffles.

Notes:

1. Pom syrup varies in concentration. I used Dynamic Health’s 100% pure pomegranate syrup, which is 5x more concentrated than pom juice.

2. In an alternate recipe calling for heavy cream and maple syrup in addition to pom syrup, the pom syrup curdled the cream. Despite the curdled cream, the ganache initially appeared to be ok: smooth, with a nice flavor. However, a few days later the flavor changed as the taste of curdled cream came through.

Meyer Lemon and Thyme Truffles

(Adapted from a recipe in Bon Appétit, December 2006)

1 1/4 cups heavy whipping cream

10 ounces high–quality bittersweet chocolate (70% cocoa), chopped, divided (I used Ghirardelli semi-sweet chips in the 2007 batch.)

1 1/2 teaspoons fresh thyme leaves, finely chopped

1 1/2 tablespoons fresh Meyer lemon juice

1 teaspoon finely chopped Meyer lemon peel (yellow part only)

1 to 1 1/2 teaspoons lemon extract

[cocoa nibs—bits of roasted shelled cocoa bits; available at specialty foods stores and at chefshop.com]

Bring cream to simmer in heavy small saucepan. Add fresh thyme leaves and remove from heat. Let steep 20 minutes (do not strain). Stir in lemon juice and peel.

While cream and thyme leaves are steeping, stir 8 ounces chocolate in double boiler or metal bowl over saucepan of simmering water until smooth. Remove from heat. Add 2 ounces chocolate; stir until smooth. Stir in cream. Chill truffle base until firm enough to roll, about 3 hours. (I needed to freeze the ganache overnight before it was firm enough to roll.)

Line rimmed baking sheet with waxed paper. Roll truffle base 2 teaspoons at a time between fingertips into balls. Transfer balls to prepared sheet and store in freezer until ready to dip. [Alternative to dipping: Truffles may be rolled in cocoa and then coated in cocoa nibs, or just coated in cocoa nibs.]

Notes:

• This ganache turned out very soft and could use some more tweaking. The original recipe calls for 9 oz. of chocolate, which I increased to 10 oz.; 11 or 12 oz. might be even better.

• The original recipe does not call for lemon extract, but the Meyer lemon flavor was so subtle that I felt it was needed. For the 2007 batch, I added 1 1/2 teaspoons, but a little less would have been fine, too. One reviewer at the Epicurious site said she added some limoncello liqueur.

Peanut Butter Truffles

Recipe courtesy Kathleen and Roger Johnson, Just Truffles, MN

Show: The Best Of

Episode: Candy

2 cups whipping cream

2 pounds white chocolate, melted

2 cups peanut butter

1 pound dark bittersweet chocolate

Crushed peanuts, for decoration

Scald cream in a small saucepan. Remove from heat and chill. Combine white chocolate and peanut butter together in a mixing bowl and blend together. Add chilled whipping cream and continue blending at high speed until a smooth consistency is achieved. Pour mixture into a suitable container and place in the refrigerator for 24 hours, to allow mixture to set.

Using a melon baller, or a number 70 scoop, form the mixture into balls. Place balls on a sheet pan and into the refrigerator to firm up.

Melt the dark chocolate over a double boiler. Gently dip each ball in the melted chocolate so that it's fully coated. Decorate with crushed peanuts. Keep truffles in the refrigerator until required.

Gingerbread Truffles

(from Bon Appetit, December 2005)

Ingredients:

3/4 cup whipping cream (I think adding a little more cream than this will make the ganache easier to handle)

10 whole allspice

10 whole cloves

1 tablespoon mild-flavored (light) molasses

1 1/2 teaspoons grated peeled fresh ginger

1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon

Pinch of salt

7 ounces plus bittersweet (not unsweetened) or semisweet chocolate, chopped

7 ounces high-quality white chocolate (such as Lindt or Perugina), chopped

crystallized ginger for garnish

Preparation:

Bring first 7 ingredients just to boil in heavy medium saucepan; remove from heat and let steep 1 hour.

Combine 7 ounces bittersweet chocolate and 7 ounces white chocolate in large metal bowl set over saucepan of simmering water; stir until chocolate is melted and smooth. Strain cream mixture and reheat to a low simmer. Add into chocolate; stir to blend. Chill filling until firm, at least 3 hours.

Line baking sheet with parchment. Using 1-inch melon baller, scoop filling and roll between palms to form balls. Place on parchment. Chill truffles at least 2 hours.

[Adapted slightly from the recipe in Bon Appétit, December 1998]

5 teaspoons instant espresso powder

3/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg

3/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon

1/4 cup crème fraîche or sour cream

Yield: about 40 truffles

Dark, Vegan Chocolate Truffles Recipe

Feel free to add a couple of tablespoons of brandy (my favourite addition) or a few drops of natural peppermint extract to the mix if you’re in to that kind of thing.

MAKES AROUND 25 TRUFFLES

300g High quality dark chocolate (70% coco solids minimum)

¼ tsp sea salt

30g / 2tbsp virgin coconut oil

230g / around 215 ml tinned coconut milk (60% coconut extract minimum. Try to get one that’s just coconut and water, without all the extra additives)

For garnish: Desiccated coconut & 100% Cocoa powder

THE NIGHT BEFORE.

Chop or break up the chocolate into smallish chunks and place in a large bowl along with the coconut oil and the salt.

Bring the coconut milk to the boil on the stove, and then pour immediately over the chocolate. Stir until the chocolate is dissolved, and the ganache is smooth and thick.

Pour into a medium sized bowl.

Cover with cling film and leave it to set overnight in the fridge (or at least 4 hours).

THE NEXT DAY.

Take two flat-ish bowls. Tip a handful of desiccated coconut into one bowl, and a few tablespoons of cocoa power in the other, making sure you’ve got enough to generously cover the bottom of each of the bowls.

Remove the ganache from the fridge.

Wash your hands and dry them well.

Use a teaspoon to scoop small amounts of ganache out of the bowl, place into the palm of your hand and as quickly as possible roll them into a ball. This does require a little skill but just keep in mind how you used to roll plasticine into balls when you were a kid and you’ll be fine.

Toss them straight into either the bowl of cocoa or coconut.

Repeat until you have rolled all the ganache into balls.

Swirl around the bowls of coconut and cocoa to ensure that each truffle is completely coated.

Wash your hands and dry them.

Carefully place each chocolate truffle onto a serving plate, cover gently with cling film and place back into the fridge to firm up again. Of course, feel free to test 1 or 2 as you go to ensure they meet your personal quality standards ;-)

Store for up to 4 days.

Shorthand: Stir 300g choc + pinch salt + 30g coconut oil + 230g hot coconut milk – smooth. Fridge overnight. Roll into balls. Swirl in cocoa powder or desiccated coconut.

Coconut Toasted Almond Truffles

A triple batch makes about 114 centers. This required less than an 8 oz. bag of coconut.

8 oz Lindt, Callebaut or Ghirardelli white chocolate (not morsels)

1 cup chopped slivered almonds, lightly toasted

1/4 cup heavy cream

2 tablespoons dark or coconut rum

1 1/2 cups finely shredded unsweetened desiccated coconut

Finely grind white chocolate in a food processor (or finely chop).

Pulse nuts in food processor until coarsely chopped.

Bring cream to a simmer in a double boiler.

Whisk in white chocolate until melted and ganache is smooth

Remove from heat and whisk in rum until well blended.

Stir in nuts.

Pour ganache into a plastic-wrap–lined 8-inch square baking pan (or use regular method, without plastic).

Cool to room temperature.

Chill, until firm, about 4 hours.

Invert ganache onto a work surface and remove plastic wrap. Cut ganache into 64 squares and roll each piece between your palms to form a ball. (or, use scooper to form centers).

When all balls are formed, roll in coconut to cover completely.

6 cups coconut, 1.5 cups nuts, triple recipe chocolate/coconut milk, half cup coconut rum, ¾ cups coconut milk

Dip

Top with more shredded coconut.

Alternative: Form centers without coconut, dip, and then roll in coconut.

Gourmet, December 2000
